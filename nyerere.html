NYERERE NATIONAL PARK

Africa’s Largest Wildlife Sanctuary

Explore one of Africa’s most untouched wilderness areas, where vast rivers, endless savannas, and remote wildlife create a true African safari experience far from the crowds.

DISCOVER THE WILDERNESS OF NYERERE

Nyerere National Park is Tanzania’s largest national park and one of the biggest protected wildlife areas in the world. Covering more than 30,000 square kilometers, it offers an unmatched sense of wilderness and isolation. Unlike northern safari circuits, Nyerere is defined by the mighty Rufiji River, which creates a rich ecosystem of waterways, lakes, and floodplains. This environment supports elephants, wild dogs, hippos, crocodiles, and hundreds of bird species. It is the perfect destination for travelers seeking a raw, authentic, and less crowded safari experience.

VISITING NYERERE NATIONAL PARK

Best Time to Visit Nyerere
June to October (Dry Season): Best for game drives and wildlife concentrations along the Rufiji River.

November to April (Green Season): Lush scenery, bird migrations, and dramatic river landscapes.

Top Things to Do in Nyerere
Boat Safaris: Cruise the Rufiji River and see hippos, crocodiles, and elephants drinking at the water’s edge.
Game Drives: Explore vast untouched plains with rich predator and herbivore populations.
Walking Safaris: Experience the wilderness on foot with expert guides in a true remote safari setting.

Unique Experience of Nyerere National Park

Nyerere National Park offers one of the most remote and untouched safari experiences in Africa. Its vast river systems, floodplains, and woodlands create a unique ecosystem where land and water wildlife coexist. It is especially famous for boat safaris along the Rufiji River and rare sightings of African wild dogs in their natural habitat.
