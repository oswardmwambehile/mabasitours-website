TARANGIRE NATIONAL PARK

The land of Giant Elephant Heards

Step into the wild beauty of Tarangire National Park, where ancient baobab trees, massive elephant herds, and diverse wildlife create an unforgettable safari experience.

EXPERIENCE THE MAGIC OF TARANGIRE

Tarangire National Park is one of Tanzania’s most underrated safari destinations, offering incredible wildlife encounters and some of the largest elephant herds in Africa. Located just two hours from Arusha, this park is famous for its scenic landscapes, diverse ecosystems, and excellent game viewing. The Tarangire River, the park’s lifeline, attracts thousands of animals, making it one of the best places to see predators, antelopes, and birdlife in action. The park’s iconic baobab trees provide a stunning backdrop, creating an atmosphere that feels truly wild and untouched.

VISITING TARANGIRE NATIONAL PARK

Best Time to Visit Tarangire
June to October (Dry Season): The best time for wildlife viewing, as animals gather around the Tarangire River. November to May (Green Season): Lush landscapes, fewer tourists, and incredible birdwatching opportunities.

Top Things to Do in Tarangire
Game Drives: Explore the vast plains and witness the park’s rich wildlife, including massive elephant herds.
Sunset Safaris: Experience the breathtaking golden sunsets over the iconic baobab-dotted landscapes.
Walking Safaris: Get closer to nature and track wildlife on foot with an experienced guide.

Unique Experience of Tarangire National Park

Tarangire National Park is known as the land of giant elephant herds and ancient baobab trees. Unlike other parks, the Tarangire River acts as a lifeline during the dry season, attracting large concentrations of wildlife. The park offers a quieter, more authentic safari experience with dramatic landscapes and incredible animal gatherings.
